## Design Context

### Users
- **Target Audience**: Luxury gift buyers and character-driven aesthetic collectors. Specifically urban youth (students, working professionals) and status-conscious gift-givers in Dhaka.
- **Job to be Done**: Selling the "curiosity factor." The user wants to own objects that spark conversations ("Where did you get that?") and reflect their unique personal taste.

### Brand Personality
- **Voice**: Sophisticated yet approachable, modern yet rooted.
- **3-Word Personality**: Confident, Playful, Cultivated.
- **Tone**: Like a stylish young professional who knows quality, has a sense of humor, and avoids generic "off-the-shelf" products. Approachable but elite in taste.

### Aesthetic Direction
- **Visual Tone**: Premium Editorial. High-end boutique feel that lets the character-driven products (duck buds, Poké Ball speakers) shine as artifacts.
- **Theme**: Single "Ivory Mode" only. Warm ivory surfaces, black typography, and gold accents.
- **Iconography**: Strictly geometric shapes and abstract icons. No animal imagery or emojis (the animal/character elements are in the products, so the UI must remain a neutral "gallery" for them).
- **References**: High-end fashion editorials, boutique gallery sites.
- **Anti-references**: Amazon-style listing grids, bright-blue "gamer" aesthetics, cluttered mass-market e-commerce.

### Design Principles
1. **Mobile-First Luxury**: Every component is designed for mobile interaction first—reachability, thumb-friendly targets, and vertical storytelling.
2. **Cinematic Motion**: Use subtle, slow transitions (ease-in-out-expo) that feel "heavy" and expensive. No bouncy or "pop" animations.
3. **Gallery Hierarchy**: The UI acts as a minimal, high-end gallery. Use generous white space and restricted color (Ivory/Black/Gold) to ensure the colorful, character-driven products are the only "loud" elements.
4. **Geometric Precision**: Avoid roundness where unnecessary; prefer sharp, intentional geometric layouts that contrast with the organic shapes of the products.
5. **Authentic Scarcity**: Design states that feel artisanal rather than mass-produced. Focus on the storytelling of individual artifacts.
